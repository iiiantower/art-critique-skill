# The uncanny

**In one plain sentence:** The uncanny is the dread that comes when the familiar turns
strange — the homely made unhomely, often through repetition, doubling, or the
lifelike-but-not-alive.

**When it helps:** Reach for it when a scene reads as familiar but slightly wrong: domestic,
recognizable, yet quietly off, leaving a low unease. It's good for work that unsettles
without depicting anything openly disturbing.

**How it gets misused:** Treating "uncanny" as a synonym for "creepy" or horror. The point
is the return of the familiar as strange, not gore or shock. It's also a culturally specific
frame, so offer it rather than impose it.

**Connects to:** the punctum (a small detail unsettling the whole), and Hammershøi's
interiors (an ordinary room turned subtly wrong).

**A question it opens:** Is there something ordinary in your work that you wanted to make
slightly unsafe?
