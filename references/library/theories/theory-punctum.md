# Punctum and studium

**In one plain sentence:** Barthes said an image holds a *studium* — its general, cultural,
readable interest — and sometimes a *punctum*, a small accidental detail that pricks you
personally and won't let go.

**When it helps:** Reach for it when a viewer is snagged by a tiny, "unimportant" detail
rather than the official subject — when something minor is doing the real emotional work,
and the piece wounds without any obvious drama. It's a good way to talk about that small
sting.

**How it gets misused:** Treating the punctum as objective or designable. It is private and
accidental, so you can't tell an artist "here is your punctum" — you can only report your
own, and ask about theirs.

**Connects to:** the uncanny (both are about a quiet detail unsettling the whole), and to
Hammershøi's interiors, where a small absence does the work.

**A question it opens:** What is the smallest thing in your work that you can't stop looking
at — and is it the thing you meant to matter?
