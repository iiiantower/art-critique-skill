# Ma — the charged interval

**In one plain sentence:** *Ma* is the meaningful emptiness between things — the pause, gap,
or interval that isn't absence but an active, charged space, carrying timing and relation.

**When it helps:** Reach for it when a work's power lives in its gaps, silences, or empty
zones, and the void feels full rather than missing — especially when a viewer holds their
breath but can't point to any object causing it.

**How it gets misused:** Flattening it into "minimalism" or "the artist left some blank
space." Ma is about interval and timing, so it implies duration and relation, not just a
visually empty patch.

**Connects to:** Rothko's color fields (power held in a breathing gap), and the punctum (both
ask you to attend to something easy to overlook).

**A question it opens:** Where is the emptiest part of your work, and what do you think is
happening in it?
