# Romanticism amid early industrialization — c. 1800–1850, Europe

**What happened:** As cities and factories spread, many Romantic artists turned toward wild
nature, ruins, and solitude — partly as a refuge from, and a reaction against, industrial
rationalization.

**Why it might matter for a critique:** It can open up why an image of vast nature might
carry loss rather than just beauty — a nostalgia for what mechanization was displacing. Use
it to ask a question, not to declare a work "about industrialization."

**Connects to:** Friedrich's *Wanderer*, and the sublime.

**A question it opens:** If this calm landscape is partly a refusal of something, what is it
turning away from?
