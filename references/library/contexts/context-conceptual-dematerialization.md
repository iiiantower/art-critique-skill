# Dematerialization of the art object — c. 1966–1972

**What happened:** A wave of conceptual practice shifted value away from the crafted object
and toward the idea, system, document, or duration behind it.

**Why it might matter for a critique:** It's useful when a work seems to withhold visual
pleasure on purpose, putting its weight on a process, a rule, or time. It can help name that
precise-but-evasive quality without calling the work "cold" or "empty."

**Connects to:** On Kawara's date paintings.

**A question it opens:** What in your work is the idea, and what is the object — and which
one are you asking me to value?
