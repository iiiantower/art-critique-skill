# Interior (Strandgade 30 series) — Vilhelm Hammershøi, c. 1898–1909

**The feeling it gives:** A cool distance and a quiet unease; the strong sense of someone
who should be there but isn't.

**The making-move:** Muted grey rooms in a very limited palette, often with a single
back-turned figure or none at all, and doors that open onto further empty rooms.

**Any contradiction:** It is present by being absent — the emptiness is what you notice, and
it feels charged rather than blank. The ordinary room is also faintly wrong.

**Why it might come to mind:** A clear case of absence felt as presence. Reach for it when a
work seems haunted by a missing person or event rather than depicting one.

**Connects to:** the uncanny (an ordinary interior turned subtly wrong), and the punctum (a
small absence doing the emotional work).

**A question it opens:** Who or what is missing from your work — and is the room waiting for
them, or done with them?
