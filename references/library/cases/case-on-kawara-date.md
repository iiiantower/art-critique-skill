# Today series (Date Paintings) — On Kawara, 1966–2013

**The feeling it gives:** A quiet heaviness with a low hum under it, and a sense of something
lost — each day marked and then gone.

**The making-move:** Each painting is only the date it was made, hand-rendered in a plain
palette; if it wasn't finished by midnight, it was destroyed. The work is a long repeated
series and carries the trace of the time spent making it.

**Any contradiction:** It is still but urgent, and sacred but utterly ordinary — a bare date
given the weight of a ritual.

**Why it might come to mind:** Use it for the move where repetition and the trace of process
turn time itself into the subject. It bridges to any work where duration or process is the
real content, whatever the imagery.

**Connects to:** Ma (charged interval and timing), and the idea of dematerialization (value
shifting from object to idea and duration).

**A question it opens:** Is the time you spent making this part of the work — and should the
viewer be able to feel it?
