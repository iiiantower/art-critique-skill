# Wanderer above the Sea of Fog — Caspar David Friedrich, c. 1818

**The feeling it gives:** Awe at a vast view, mixed with the sense of being held at a
distance from it — summoned and shut out at the same time.

**The making-move:** A figure stands with his back to us on a peak, blocking our entry, so
we look *with* him into a haze that dissolves any measurable distance.

**Any contradiction:** It feels grand and yet precarious — the lone figure is tiny against
something that could swallow him.

**Why it might come to mind:** Use it not for "mountains" but for that double pull of awe
and distance — a vastness that invites and excludes at once. That lets it jump to any work
that makes a viewer feel both called in and kept out, regardless of subject.

**Connects to:** the sublime (awe shading into being dwarfed), and Rothko (overwhelm that
needs no depicted scene).

**A question it opens:** Does your work let me in, or does it put a figure's back between me
and the view?
