# No. 14, 1960 — Mark Rothko, 1960

**The feeling it gives:** A held breath; a heaviness that somehow also hums, pulling you to
stand close and stay.

**The making-move:** Two saturated fields meet at a soft, breathing edge, with a drastically
limited palette and a scale that sets your viewing distance. Nothing is depicted.

**Any contradiction:** It is still on the surface but urgent underneath — motionless, yet it
won't let you go.

**Why it might come to mind:** This is a useful bridge: pure feeling with no subject at all.
Bring it in to test whether a representational work's power actually comes from its colour
and intervals rather than from its image.

**Connects to:** Ma (power held in a breathing gap), and the sublime (overwhelm without a
scene).

**A question it opens:** If we removed every recognizable object from your work, would the
feeling survive on colour and interval alone?
