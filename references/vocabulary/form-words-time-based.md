# Form Words — time-based work (audio / video / performance)

Plain names for things the maker actually did **over time** — to sound, motion, pacing, and
the relation between what you hear and what you see. Useful for connecting works that share a
making-move in time, and for linking to ideas about form. Like the visual palette, this can
feel cold on its own, so pair it with the feeling it produces.

This palette is for **time-based** work (music, sound, film, video, performance). For the
still/visual side of a piece, use `form-words.md`; a video usually needs both together.

Say it in a sentence; these are prompts for noticing, not labels to paste.

- **a held pause / silence that works** — an empty stretch doing real work (the time-based cousin of load-bearing empty space)
- **a sudden shift in pace** — an abrupt change of tempo or energy
- **repetition / a loop** — a repeated phrase, beat, gesture, or shot
- **a build and release** — tension accumulated then let go
- **an abrupt cut** — a hard edit or break that severs the flow
- **a long take / held duration** — refusing to cut, making you sit in time
- **a shift in dynamics** — loud against soft, dense against sparse
- **sound and image pulling apart** — what you hear and what you see don't match (or are deliberately synced)
- **a drone or sustain** — a continuous tone or held state under everything
- **layering of voices / tracks** — simultaneous strands stacked in time
- **rhythm against the grid** — playing with, ahead of, or behind an expected beat
- **a disorienting sense of time** — duration that feels stretched, compressed, or looped
- **the trace of the live moment** — evidence of real-time presence, error, or the body performing
- **restaging a known piece or form** — covering, sampling, or quoting a recognizable work or convention
