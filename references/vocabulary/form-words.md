# Form Words — visual / static work

Plain names for things the maker actually did to the surface, the composition, or the
material. Useful for connecting works that share a making-move, and for linking to ideas
about form. Form-talk can feel cold on its own, so pair it with the feeling it produces.

This palette is for **visual or static** work (painting, drawing, photo, print, sculpture,
still installation). For audio/video/performance, use `form-words-time-based.md` instead;
for video, both palettes apply together.

Say it in a sentence; these are prompts for noticing, not labels to paste.

- **load-bearing empty space** — the void or gap is doing real work
- **a sudden shift in colour temperature** — an abrupt warm/cool break
- **no centre** — weight pushed to the edges or off the frame
- **depth through haze** — things desaturate and soften toward the distance
- **flattening** — illusion of depth collapsed onto the surface
- **repetition / a series** — a repeated unit or serial structure
- **a severing crop** — framing that cuts the subject off hard
- **visible, bodily mark-making** — gestural marks that show the hand
- **meaning by removal** — scraping, hiding, erasing as the method
- **disorienting scale** — size relations that don't add up
- **an impossible or withheld vantage** — the implied viewpoint can't really exist
- **layers and veiling** — translucent layers, partial covering
- **crisp against dissolved edges** — sharp boundaries set against soft ones
- **a drastically limited palette** — very few colours on purpose
- **the trace of process or time** — physical evidence of how/when it was made
- **restaging a known composition** — borrowing a recognizable arrangement from another work
