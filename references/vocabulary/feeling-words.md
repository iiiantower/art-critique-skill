# Feeling Words

A small palette of plain words for the bodily or emotional response a work gives you,
*before* you can explain it. This is the most useful starting point for association,
because two works can share a feeling even when their subjects have nothing in common.

These are here to help you NOTICE and NAME a feeling, not to be pasted as codes. Say them
in a real sentence ("it makes me hold my breath"), and feel free to use your own words.

- **holding your breath** — a charged stillness; you instinctively go quiet
- **lightness / floating** — buoyant, ungrounded, lifted
- **heaviness** — sinking, weighed down, a downward pull
- **losing your footing** — dizziness, instability, being pulled in or over
- **a small sting** — something sharp jabs your attention
- **airlessness** — pressed, crowded, no way out
- **tenderness** — soft warmth, care, something fragile you want to protect
- **intimacy** — being let into something close and private
- **being kept at a distance** — observed but not touched; a cool remove
- **the feeling of absence** — someone or something missing, felt as a presence
- **quiet unease** — a low wrongness you can't yet name
- **awe** — scale beyond you; humbled
- **bright play** — quick pleasure, mischief, delight
- **blankness / overexposure** — flat, numbed, too much light or sameness
- **a quiet hum** — sustained energy that is alive but still
- **rawness** — an exposed nerve, unguarded, too much feeling at once
