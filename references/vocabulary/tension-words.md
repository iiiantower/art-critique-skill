# Tension Words

Plain ways to name a contradiction in the work — two things that shouldn't sit together but
do. A contradiction is one of the strongest things to follow, because it makes you reach for
an idea that can put a name to the gap.

Say it as a sentence: "it feels X and Y at the same time." Use your own phrasing freely.

- **tender and violent at once** — closeness and harm in the same gesture
- **still on the surface, urgent underneath** — frozen image, pressure inside it
- **beautiful but troubling** — seductive form carrying uneasy content
- **present by being absent** — something foregrounded precisely because it's missing
- **familiar but slightly wrong** — the everyday tipped into strangeness
- **grand but fragile** — huge in scale, precarious in substance
- **orderly but on the verge of collapse** — a visible system about to break down
- **inviting but cold** — draws you in while withholding warmth
- **funny but grieving** — humour carrying loss underneath
- **precise but evasive** — tightly controlled, yet refusing to say what it means
- **sacred but ordinary** — ritual weight attached to something trivial
- **contained but overflowing** — a frame or format that can't hold its own content
