# {title} — {artist}, {year}

**The feeling it gives:** Write one or two sentences in plain language about the bodily or
emotional response, in the spirit of `vocabulary/feeling-words.md`.

**The making-move:** One or two sentences on what the artist actually did (form, surface,
composition, timing, sound, any borrowing), in the spirit of the form palette for the medium —
`vocabulary/form-words.md` for visual/static work, `vocabulary/form-words-time-based.md` for
audio/video/performance.

**Any contradiction:** If there's a "this and that at once" tension, name it in a sentence.
Otherwise skip.

**Why it might come to mind:** A few sentences explaining what felt or structural quality
lets this jump to OTHER, unrelated works — and whether the connection is something an artist
would have put in deliberately (a borrowing) or something a viewer projects.

**Connects to:** name a couple of related cards or ideas in the library and say, in a phrase,
why they connect.

**A question it opens:** One open question you could hand back to an artist. Not a conclusion.
