# Demo Walkthrough (demo video with narration)

A first-reading framework for when the artist sends a **short recorded walkthrough** — they
show the work and talk through it at the same time. Use it **after** the Ingest section in
`notes.md` is complete (observed / stated / alignment separated). Pair with `four-steps.md`
for still-frame detail and `time-based-media.md` for pacing and sound.

Keep notes short; skip what you cannot verify.

## Work — what the piece shows (from frames, not from speech)
What the work itself is: materials, form, colour, motion, sound made by the work. What you
notice first on screen. What stays with you visually or sonically **independent of what the
artist says**.

## Presentation — how it is shown (camera, light, demo gesture)
How the artist films or presents it: framing, movement, lighting, what's in the background,
gestures while demonstrating. Ask yourself: is this layer part of the work, or just how you're
being shown it?

## Speech — what the artist said (their perspective)
What they name, emphasise, or explain — with timestamps or quotes where it matters. This is
**stated**, not automatically **true of the work**.

## Gap — where speech and image align or diverge
Where narration and frames match; where they pull apart (said X, saw Y; pointed here, looked
different). These gaps are often the best seeds for the AGENDA.

## Judge — your first take (brief)
What already holds you; what confuses you; what you would want Round 1 to clarify. Do not
treat the walkthrough as the final meaning of the work.
