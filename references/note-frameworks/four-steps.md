# Four Steps in Art Criticism (default for visual / static work)

This is the default first-reading framework for **visual or static** work. It is pluggable —
see `README.md` to swap it; for audio/video/performance use `time-based-media.md` instead.
Use it to find your feelings and a basic footing, not to fill in every blank. Work one
section at a time. Keep each note short (under ~100 words); skip or flag anything you can't
answer yet.

## Describe — tell what you see (the visual facts)
Artist, kind of work, title, date, and the literal things in it (if known). What you notice
first and why. Colors, shapes and their edges, lines, textures, light, time of day, and the
overall mood.

## Analyze — how the parts work together
How color, shape, line, texture, and light are used, and to what effect. Which principles of
design organize the elements. How the mood is achieved through these choices, and how the
maker's tools create a particular look or focus.

## Interpret — what it might mean
What you think the work is trying to say, what it means to you, and how it connects to your
life. What feelings it raises, whether anything stands for something else, and why the maker
might have made these choices.

## Judge — your own evaluation
Where you find value or worth (beauty, an idea, a feeling, a social message, a fresh way of
seeing). Whether it would matter to others. Where it falls short and why — and be honest
that your own taste and bias are part of the judgment. Give both positive and negative.
