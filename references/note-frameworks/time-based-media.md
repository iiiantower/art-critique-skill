# Four Steps for Time-based Media (audio / video / performance)

A framework for work that unfolds **over time** — music, sound, film, video, performance.
It keeps the same Describe → Analyze → Interpret → Judge spine as `four-steps.md`, but the
prompts attend to time, sound, motion, and the relation between them. Use it to find your
feelings and a basic footing, not to fill in every blank. Keep each note short; skip or flag
what you can't answer yet. For the still/visual frames of a video, `four-steps.md` also helps.

## Describe — what happens, over time
What kind of work and how long it runs (if known). What you notice first and when. The broad
shape across its duration (beginning / middle / end, or sections). What you hear (voices,
sound, music, silence) and what you see (if any), and roughly how they sit together in time.

## Analyze — how the parts work in time
How pace, rhythm, repetition, dynamics, and pauses are used, and to what effect. How sound
and image relate (synced, pulling apart, one leading). What holds it together or breaks it
(cuts, builds, loops, long takes). How the overall mood is achieved through these choices.

## Interpret — what it might mean
What you think the work is trying to say, what it means to you, how it connects to your life.
What feelings its unfolding raises, whether anything stands for something else, and why the
maker might have shaped time and sound this way.

## Judge — your own evaluation
Where you find value or worth (a feeling, an idea, a way of using time or sound, a social
message). Whether it would matter to others. Where it falls short and why — and be honest
that your own taste and bias are part of the judgment. Give both positive and negative.
